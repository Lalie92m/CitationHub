Citation Guide

Source Type Description Key Citation Elements
Journal Article Scholarly articles published in academic journals, often peer-reviewed. Author(s), year, article title, journal name, volume, issue, page numbers, DOI or URL
Conference Paper Papers presented at academic or professional conferences. Author(s), year, paper title, conference name, location, page numbers (if available), DOI or URL
Thesis/Dissertation Academic research submitted in support of a degree (e.g., Master's or PhD). Author, year, title (italicized), type of thesis, university, repository URL (if online)
Website Content found on web pages including articles, blogs, or organizational content. Author/organization, year (or "n.d."), title, site name, URL
Newspaper Article News reports from print or online newspapers. Author, date, article title, newspaper name, page number (if in print), URL (if online)
Magazine Article Informative or entertaining articles for the general public. Author, date, title, magazine name, volume(issue), page numbers or URL
Report Formal documents by governments, NGOs, or institutions. Authoring body, year, title (italicized), report number (if any), publisher, URL
Patent Legal document granting exclusive rights to an invention. Inventor(s), year, title, patent number, issuing authority
Dataset Raw data used for research or analysis. Author(s), year, title, version, repository name, DOI or URL
Interview Spoken content from experts or individuals (may be personal or published). Interviewee, year, type (personal/published), interviewer, date, platform (if applicable)
Manuscript Unpublished academic or creative work. Author(s), year, title (italicized), status (e.g., unpublished manuscript), institution or source
Audio/Visual Videos, podcasts, films, music recordings, etc. Creator(s), year, title, format (e.g., [Film], [Podcast]), platform or publisher, URL
Book Published long-form works, fiction or non-fiction. Author(s), year, title (italicized), edition (if not 1st), publisher
